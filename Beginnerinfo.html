Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, programs, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft. Think of it as digital safety for people, companies, and systems.

2. Key Concepts Confidentiality: Ensuring that sensitive information is accessed only by authorized people. Integrity: Making sure data is accurate and not tampered with. Availability: Ensuring systems and data are accessible to authorized users when needed. Authentication: Verifying the identity of users or devices before granting access. Authorization: Controlling what an authenticated user can do or access.

3. Common Cyber Threats Malware: Software designed to harm your computer or steal information (e.g., viruses, ransomware, spyware). Phishing: Fraudulent emails or messages tricking you into giving personal information. Ransomware: Malware that locks your files or system and demands payment for release. Man-in-the-Middle Attacks (MitM): Intercepting communication between two parties to steal information. Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks: Overloading systems to make them unavailable. Weak Passwords: Easy-to-guess passwords can allow hackers to access accounts.

4. Basic Cybersecurity Best Practices Use Strong Passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols; avoid obvious passwords. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security beyond passwords. Keep Software Updated: Updates often patch security vulnerabilities. Be Cautious with Emails/Links: Don’t click suspicious links or download unknown attachments. Backup Your Data: Regular backups protect against ransomware or accidental loss. Secure Your Wi-Fi: Use strong encryption (WPA3/WPA2) and a complex password. Install Security Software: Use antivirus and anti-malware programs. Monitor Accounts: Regularly check for unusual activity in your accounts.
